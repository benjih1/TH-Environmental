Habitat and water quality improvement at Lingley Mere Business Park

Two surface water fed ponds in the centre of the business park suffered from poor water quality, algae blooms and were experiencing fish deaths especially during the warmer months. Through consultation with the facilities management company I instigated the following management practice changes:

  • Reduced flora clearing in the green space surrounding the watercourse and stopped the practice completely in the last 1 metre up to the waters edge.
  • Altered the timings and speed of the recirculating pumps to bolster DO levels during high risk periods.
  • Advised on the purchase of an emergency fine bubble aeration system.
  • Advised on the purchase and installation of 10 BioHaven floating treatment wetlands to increase nutrient uptake from the ponds, aid in oxygen diffusion and provide the fish safe havens.
  • As the project proceeded the smaller pond was given up to a nearby housing estate for surface water drainage mitigation this posed a risk to the fish present so I removed and placed them into the larger pond.
